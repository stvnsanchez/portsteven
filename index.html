Journal

WorldTech Information Solution

Visiting Worldtech Information Solutions in Davao helped me gain clarity about my career path as a third-year IT student who was unsure whether to focus on programming, networking, security, or cloud computing. The career counselors explained the importance of IT certifications such as ITIL, CompTIA, and Cisco, emphasizing that these are globally recognized proofs of skills, not just certificates. Hearing success stories of students who were able to work in countries like Singapore and Australia made these career goals feel more realistic and achievable.

Discovering CodeChum

Discovering CodeChum has greatly improved my programming studies as a student. This Philippine-based platform, created by Cebuano programmers, fits well with the needs of Filipino learners. What makes CodeChum special is its instant feedback, allowing me to know right away if my code works, which helps me learn faster and improve my solutions. It supports self-paced learning, offers practical programming problems, and allows me to practice multiple languages like C, Python, and Java in one place. Because of this, my problem-solving and debugging skills have improved, and I have become more confident and better prepared for real-world programming.

Industry Exposure at Rivan School of Technology

Visiting Rivan IT (Rivan School of Technology) showed me what professional IT training is like beyond the classroom. Seeing their labs with real Cisco routers, switches, and enterprise equipment made me realize how important hands-on experience is in preparing for real work. The instructors shared how their global careers were made possible through proper training and certifications, and they emphasized that Filipino IT professionals are highly respected worldwide if they have the right skills and credentials. This visit helped me understand that while college provides a strong foundation, specialized training and certifications in areas like networking, cybersecurity, and cloud computing are essential to become industry-ready and competitive.

MATA Technologies VR Demo

Experiencing MATA Technologies’ VR and 360° tour demo gave me a glimpse of the future of technology beyond websites and mobile apps. Their virtual tour of Lubang Island made me feel like I was really there, showing how VR can be used in industries like tourism and real estate. I was inspired by how they use technology to preserve Philippine culture, support local businesses, and showcase Filipino innovation. This experience helped me realize that fields like VR, AR, and AI are real career paths and that technology can be used not only for entertainment but also to solve real-world problems and create global impact.

CDRRMO Command Center & E-911 Service

Visiting the CDRRMO in Bohol helped me understand how technology and preparedness play an important role in disaster management. The team explained how they monitor weather conditions, respond to emergencies, and coordinate with different agencies to ensure public safety. Seeing their operations made me realize how planning, teamwork, and the use of technology are essential in reducing disaster risks and protecting communities. This experience showed me that IT skills can also be applied in public service, especially in disaster response and risk management, where accurate information and quick communication can save lives.
